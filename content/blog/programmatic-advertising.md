<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-what-is-programmatic-advertising">What is programmatic advertising?</h2>

<p>“Programmatic” ad buying typically refers to the use of software to purchase digital advertising, instead of the traditional process that involves RFPs, human negotiations, and manual insertion orders. It’s using machines to buy ads. Canadian Website Designs can help you determine what programmatic advertising is right for you. </p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Why does programmatic advertising matter?</h2>

<p>Before programmatic ad buying, digital ads were bought and sold by human ad buyers and salespeople, who can be expensive and unreliable. Programmatic advertising introduced efficiency. It promises to make the ad buying system more efficient, and therefore cheaper, by removing humans from the process wherever possible. Canadian Website Designs will be able to introduce you to this type of advertising. </p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">So robots are replacing people? Great.</h2>

<p>Yes and no. Technology is being used to replace some of the more menial tasks that humans have historically had to handle, but they’re still required to optimize campaigns and to plan strategies. Programmatic technology will probably mean there are fewer ad buyers in the world, but it could also allow both marketers and sellers to spend more of their time planning sophisticated, customized campaigns instead of getting bogged down in bureaucracy. Canadian Website Designs will ensure that your programmatic advertising can optimize your next campaign. </p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Is programmatic buying is the same as <a href="https://digiday.com/platforms/what-is-real-time-bidding/">real-time bidding</a>, then?</h2>

<p>No, it’s not. Real-time bidding is a type of programmatic ad buying, but it isn’t the only one. RTB refers to the purchase of ads through real-time auctions, but programmatic software also allows advertisers to buy guaranteed ad impressions in advance from specific publisher sites. This method of buying is often referred to as “programmatic direct.”</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Is programmatic “the future of ad buying”?</h2>

<p>Yes. It’s impossible to tell what portion of advertising is now traded programmatically, but it’s definitely on the rise. Some agencies now say they’re eager to buy as much media as possible through programmatic channels, and some major brands have even built out in-house teams to handle their programmatic ad buying as they spend more of their marketing budgets that way. At the moment, it’s mainly online ads that are traded programmatically, but increasingly media companies and agencies are exploring ways to sell “traditional” media this way, including TV spots and out-of-home ads.<br> <br></p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Understanding the Basics: What is Programmatic Advertising?</strong></h2>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>The Core Mechanism</strong></h3>

<p>At its core, Programmatic Advertising refers to the automated buying and selling of online ad impressions through real-time auctions. Instead of pre-purchasing inventory, ads are bought and placed automatically, using algorithms to determine where they appear and to whom they are shown.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Real-Time Bidding vs. Programmatic Direct</strong></h3>

<p>While many associate Programmatic Advertising with Real-Time Bidding (RTB), it's essential to distinguish between the two. RTB is a type of programmatic buying, whereas Programmatic Direct involves purchasing specific inventory in advance.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Why Programmatic Advertising is the Future</strong></h2>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Efficiency and Precision</strong></h3>

<p>Gone are the days of manual placements and negotiations. With Programmatic Advertising, advertisers can target audiences with remarkable precision, ensuring that their message reaches the right people at the right time.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Data-Driven Insights</strong></h3>

<p>Relying on vast amounts of data, this approach offers insights that traditional methods can't match. Advertisers gain a deeper understanding of their audiences and can adjust campaigns in real-time.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Cost-Effective</strong></h3>

<p>Not only does it streamline the process, but Programmatic Advertising also often results in cost savings, as it eliminates the middleman and reduces wasted impressions.</p>
